The Verge’s 2022 holiday gift guide for working from home

Elevate your workspace aesthetic or just become a more productive human with our work-from-home gift guide.

By Alice Jovanée

Updated Dec 15, 2022, 10:12 PM UTC

Share this story

I don’t think working from home is what Dolly Parton had in mind when she penned the immortal lyrics of “9 to 5,” but the home office is here to stay. That’s why we’ve consulted Verge staffers and collected a handful of essentials and nice-to-haves that will turn any home office into a nicer place for you to spend a third of your day, regardless of your job or taste when it comes to all things tech.

Our WFH recommendations range from fancy fundamentals like the Jarvis Bamboo Standing Desk ($530) and Apple’s latest MacBook Air ($1,049) to inexpensive items that lean heavily on aesthetics, such as a collection of attractive desk mats from KBDfans ($15) and a genuine leather mousepad from Nomad ($85). We’ve even collected a few items designed to keep your health in check — both physical and otherwise.

So whether you need a suggestion for yourself or want some gift ideas for making the workspace of another juuuust a little extra this holiday season, we’ve got your back.

Every laptop or desktop setup deserves a fun desk mat. They're comfier on your palms and mouse and even make your keyboard sound better. The Leuchtturm1917 might be tough to pronounce, but it's easy on the eyes. It's also available in more than 20 different colors and can be personalized with your own embossed message. Anker 341 USB-C Hub

USB-C hubs are an essential accessory for anyone using a laptop with a limited port selection. Laptops like Apple's latest MacBook Air feature just two USB-C ports, but with Anker's 341 USB-C Hub, you can expand that selection drastically. The expansive peripheral only occupies a single USB-C port but gives you two USB-C and USB-A ports, an HDMI output, and an SD card reader in return.

They say that the strongest steel is forged inside of this poor dog's house. The "This Is Fine" dog has become an endearing (and occasionally macabre) mascot of chaos, entropy, and the inevitable heat death of the universe. This Funko Pop figure of the "This Is Fine" dog is a great deskbound reminder of how to keep your cool, even when your house is literally on fire.

Lamy Safari

We all know someone who is a sucker for fancy pens, and the Lamy Safari is a refillable fountain pen that's so fancy, you'll find an excuse to make out a check. If you want to sound better on Zoom calls or simply record better audio, the Blue Yeti Nano is a great place to start. The mic pairs with your laptop or desktop via a single USB cable and features a built-in 3.5mm audio jack, too, if you need to preview what you'll sound like.

The shiny router is great if your internet speed is less than 1 gigabit per second, and it can easily pair with up to three units for up to 6,600 square feet of coverage. Best of all, it provides support for Wi-Fi 6E, Thread, and Matter, letting you futureproof your smart home.

Herman Miller Sayl Chair
